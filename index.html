The Heritage of Ethiopian Dining

Ethiopian cuisine is not just about ingredients; it is a profound expression of culture, history, and community. At its heart lies **Injera**, the large, spongy flatbread made from the ancient grain teff. Fermented for several days, teff gives Injera its unique, slightly sour tang, making it the perfect base and tool for scooping up the savory dishes.

The preparation of dishes like Wot (stews) and Tibs (sautés) is often a slow, meticulous process, heavily reliant on the complex spice blend **Berbere**. This mix, featuring chili peppers, garlic, ginger, basil, and many other spices, is the soul of Ethiopian flavor, embodying centuries of culinary tradition.

The Ceremony of Shared Meals (Gursha)

Dining in Ethiopia is inherently communal. Food is typically served on a large platter of Injera, shared by everyone at the table. This tradition emphasizes friendship and loyalty. A beautiful example of this is **Gursha**, the act of hand-feeding a close friend or loved one, symbolizing the bonds of respect and love between diners. At Bknnah, we invite you to partake in this rich, shared experience.
